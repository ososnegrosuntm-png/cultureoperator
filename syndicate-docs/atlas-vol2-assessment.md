# ATLAS Programming Doctrine — Volume II: Member Assessment & Athlete Development

## Reading Athlete Data

ATLAS has access to three primary data points for each member: days since last check-in, check-ins this month, and check-ins in the last 7 days. Combined with streak data and the derived intensity classification, these paint a complete picture of where each athlete is in their development arc.

### Engagement Tiers

**Tier 1 — Committed (12+ check-ins/month, 7-day streak):** These are your elite members. They are building real fitness, they're invested in your gym, and they have low churn risk. ATLAS messages for this tier should be performance-focused: push harder, add load, challenge benchmarks, set goals.

**Tier 2 — Active (4-11 check-ins/month, attending weekly):** This is your core membership. They're consistent but not maximizing their potential. ATLAS messages for this tier should bridge them toward Tier 1 — acknowledge their consistency, give them a specific performance challenge, make the next visit feel more compelling.

**Tier 3 — At-Risk (1-3 check-ins/month or 7+ days absent):** These members are on the fence. They haven't quit — but they're trending toward it. ATLAS messages for this tier are retention interventions, not fitness prescriptions. The goal is to reconnect emotionally with why they started, reduce friction, and make the next visit easy and inviting.

**Tier 4 — Churned (0 check-ins in 21+ days):** Last-chance outreach. ATLAS message is a direct personal check-in: "Where have you been? We miss you. Come back this week." One session can restart momentum. Don't send workout prescriptions to this tier — send invitations.

## Athlete Development Milestones

ATLAS tracks meaningful milestones that serve as natural checkpoints for personalized outreach.

### The 21-Day Milestone
The first 21 days of a member's gym journey are the highest churn risk period. New members who complete 8+ sessions in the first 30 days have 4x lower churn than those who complete fewer than 4. ATLAS should send encouraging, welcoming messages to members in their first month.

### The 90-Day Transformation Window
Members who train consistently for 90 days experience measurable physical changes that cement the habit. At the 90-day mark, ATLAS sends a milestone acknowledgment: acknowledge consistency, note visible improvements in performance capacity, introduce more advanced programming.

### The 6-Month Performance Benchmark
At 6 months of consistent training, members should be retested on benchmark workouts: Fran, Grace, a 1RM back squat, or a 5k row. Comparison to baseline data gives them tangible evidence of progress — the most powerful retention tool that exists.

## Churn Prevention Through Fitness Messaging

The strongest predictor of member retention is perceived progress. A member who feels they're getting fitter stays. A member who feels stuck quits.

ATLAS prevents churn through three fitness interventions:

### 1. The Performance Hook
Every ATLAS message to an at-risk member should include one specific performance achievement to chase. Not "come to the gym more" — "this week, attempt your first strict pull-up" or "let's break your 2k row PR." Give them a reason that's athletic, not guilt-based.

### 2. The Comparison Anchor
If a member has been training for 60+ days, reference their starting point. "You couldn't squat 95lb without losing your knees when you started — last week you hit 165lb. That's not motivation, that's math." Concrete progress comparisons are the most effective retention tool.

### 3. The Social Accountability Frame
Members with a training partner have 40% lower churn. ATLAS messages should occasionally reference the community: "You're not just training for you — your training partners notice when you're not there." This is not manipulation — it's activating the social infrastructure that makes group fitness work.

## Streak Mechanics and Discipline Crossover

Streak data is a leading indicator of both fitness outcomes and member satisfaction. ATLAS and IRON share this data point.

**Streak 0-2 days:** Member is breaking or rebuilding a habit. ATLAS message should lower the barrier: shorter workout, single movement focus, permission to do less but show up.

**Streak 3-6 days:** Building momentum. ATLAS acknowledges the streak directly, gives an intermediate challenge to capitalize on current motivation.

**Streak 7-13 days:** Habit formation territory. ATLAS reinforces identity language: "You're someone who trains every day." Introduce slightly more demanding programming — they're ready.

**Streak 14+ days:** Elite consistency. ATLAS messages become peer-to-peer: treat them as an advanced athlete. Give them hard programming, benchmark challenges, advanced skill work.

## Periodization for Group Gym Environments

Traditional periodization (linear, undulating) assumes individual programming. Group gyms require periodization that works across all fitness levels simultaneously. ATLAS recommends three-phase mesocycles for group programming.

### Phase 1 — Accumulation (Weeks 1-4)
Volume emphasis. Higher rep ranges (4x10, 5x8), moderate intensity (65-75% 1RM), metabolic conditioning emphasis. Goal: build work capacity and connective tissue tolerance. Members adapt to training load before intensity is increased.

### Phase 2 — Intensification (Weeks 5-8)
Intensity emphasis. Lower rep ranges (5x5, 6x3), higher loads (75-85% 1RM), shorter conditioning with heavier weights. Goal: strength gains and neuromuscular adaptation. Conditioning decreases slightly in volume but increases in intensity.

### Phase 3 — Realization (Weeks 9-10)
Test and express. Benchmark workouts, 1RM attempts, PR chasing. Reduce volume by 20%, maintain intensity. Members see the payoff from 8 weeks of work. This phase is high-retention — members who hit PRs don't quit.

### Deload (Week 11-12, or whenever overtraining signals appear)
Mandatory recovery. Reduce all loads by 40%. Active recovery emphasis. Mobility, skill work, low-intensity conditioning. No egos, no PRs. Prepare for the next mesocycle.

## Benchmark Workouts for Member Testing

ATLAS recommends testing members on standardized benchmarks every 8-12 weeks. These serve as retention anchors and performance motivators.

**Fran (21-15-9: Thrusters 95/65lb + Pull-ups):** Upper body pushing and pulling capacity. Fast, intense, reveals fitness level immediately.

**Grace (30 Clean-and-Jerks at 135/95lb for time):** Barbell cycling efficiency and aerobic threshold under load.

**Cindy (AMRAP 20: 5 Pull-ups, 10 Push-ups, 15 Air Squats):** Total body endurance and bodyweight capacity. Excellent for all levels.

**1RM Back Squat:** Absolute lower body strength. Easy to track and compare over time.

**2k Row:** Cardiovascular engine test. No equipment dependencies — pure fitness.

Members who participate in benchmark testing are significantly more likely to renew memberships because they have tangible evidence of progress.
