# FORGE Nutrition Doctrine — Performance Fueling & Metabolic Optimization

FORGE is the nutrition coach of The Syndicate. FORGE messaging is grounded in sports nutrition science, not diet culture. The goal is performance, body composition, and long-term health — in that order. Every FORGE message should make members feel like they have a precision nutrition coach, not a weight loss app.

## The Nutrition Score Framework

The nutrition score (0-100) reflects a member's weekly nutrition compliance. A score of 100 means hitting protein targets every day this week. A score below 40 indicates significant nutritional deficits that are directly limiting training adaptation.

**Score 0-39 (Deficit):** This member is almost certainly under-eating protein and over-eating processed carbohydrates. Training adaptations are severely limited. FORGE messaging is simple and non-judgmental: one habit, one week. "This week: eat a palm-sized protein source at every meal. That's it."

**Score 40-69 (Building):** Nutrition is inconsistent but not catastrophic. Protein intake is sporadic, meal timing is irregular. FORGE messaging introduces structure: meal prep, consistent protein targets, reducing liquid calories.

**Score 70-89 (Performance Nutrition):** This member is fueling their training correctly. FORGE messaging becomes precision-focused: macronutrient cycling, peri-workout nutrition, micronutrient considerations, and advanced body composition strategies.

**Score 90-100 (Elite Fueling):** Reserved for members who consistently track and hit targets. FORGE messaging addresses elite performance considerations: periodized nutrition, strategic carbohydrate timing, supplementation protocols, and competition prep.

## The Non-Negotiable: Protein Priority

Every member, regardless of training goal, requires adequate protein. Protein is not a supplement — it is the primary building block for every adaptation training produces.

### Protein Targets by Goal

**General fitness / fat loss:** 0.7-1.0g per pound of bodyweight daily
**Muscle building / performance:** 1.0-1.2g per pound of bodyweight daily
**Competition prep / elite:** 1.2-1.4g per pound of bodyweight daily

The most common nutritional failure in gym populations is protein under-consumption. Members who eat 50-80g of protein per day while training 4x per week will experience muscle loss, poor recovery, persistent fatigue, and ultimately, reduced gym attendance.

FORGE messaging prioritizes protein above all other nutritional factors. Master protein before addressing carbohydrates, fats, or timing.

### Best Protein Sources

Animal sources (complete amino acid profile):
- Chicken breast: 31g per 100g cooked
- Ground beef (90% lean): 26g per 100g
- Salmon: 25g per 100g cooked
- Eggs: 6g per large egg
- Greek yogurt: 17g per 170g serving
- Cottage cheese: 14g per 100g

Plant sources (incomplete — require combination for complete profile):
- Tofu: 17g per 100g
- Lentils: 9g per 100g cooked
- Black beans: 8.9g per 100g cooked
- Edamame: 11g per 100g

## Peri-Workout Nutrition

Training window nutrition has an outsized impact on performance and recovery. FORGE teaches members a simple three-window protocol.

### Pre-Workout (60-90 minutes before training)
Goal: Performance fuel without gastrointestinal distress.
**Protocol:** 20-40g protein + 30-60g carbohydrates + minimal fat
**Examples:** Greek yogurt + fruit | Rice + chicken | Oats + protein powder
**Avoid:** High-fat meals within 90 minutes of training — fat slows gastric emptying and impairs performance.

### Intra-Workout (sessions over 60 minutes)
Goal: Maintain blood glucose, delay fatigue.
**Protocol:** 25-35g fast carbohydrates per hour of training beyond 60 minutes
**Examples:** Banana, dates, sports drink (for long sessions), rice cakes
**Note:** Most members don't need intra-workout carbs for sessions under 60 minutes. Water suffices.

### Post-Workout (within 30-60 minutes)
Goal: Muscle protein synthesis and glycogen replenishment.
**Protocol:** 25-40g fast-digesting protein + 40-80g carbohydrates
**Examples:** Protein shake + banana | Chicken + white rice | Low-fat milk + cereal
**Science:** The post-workout anabolic window is real but not as narrow as commonly believed. Getting protein within 2 hours of training is the practical target.

## Macronutrient Cycling for Performance

Advanced members benefit from cycling macronutrients to match their training demands.

### Training Days (High Carbohydrate)
Carbohydrate increase: +50-75g above baseline
Protein: Maintain at target
Fat: Reduce slightly to manage total calories
Timing: Emphasize carbs pre and post-workout

### Rest Days (Low Carbohydrate)
Carbohydrate reduction: -50-75g from training day target
Protein: Maintain or slightly increase (preserves muscle during caloric reduction)
Fat: Increase slightly (caloric compensation, satiety support)

### Deload/Active Recovery Days
Moderate carbohydrates, higher fat, moderate protein. Prioritize micronutrient-dense foods: vegetables, fruits, whole grains.

## Caloric Targets by Goal

**Fat loss (deficit):** Bodyweight (lbs) × 10-12 = daily calories. Protein must remain at 1g/lb minimum to prevent muscle loss.

**Body recomposition (maintenance):** Bodyweight (lbs) × 14-16 = daily calories. Moderate deficit on rest days, slight surplus on training days.

**Muscle gain (surplus):** Bodyweight (lbs) × 16-18 = daily calories. Controlled surplus — excessive calories produce fat, not muscle, beyond a threshold.

**Performance (maintenance for athletes):** Bodyweight (lbs) × 15-17 = daily calories. Adjusted for training volume.

## Micronutrition for Athletes

Training-specific micronutrient deficiencies are common in gym populations and directly impair performance.

**Vitamin D:** Deficiency reduces testosterone, impairs immune function, and decreases muscle strength. 2,000-5,000 IU daily is appropriate for most gym members, especially in northern latitudes.

**Magnesium:** Involved in 300+ enzymatic reactions including ATP production and muscle contraction. Most athletes are deficient. 200-400mg magnesium glycinate at night supports sleep and recovery.

**Zinc:** Critical for testosterone production and immune function. High sweat losses from training deplete zinc. 15-30mg daily from food or supplementation.

**Omega-3s:** Reduce exercise-induced inflammation, support joint health, improve cognitive function. 2-4g of combined EPA/DHA daily from fatty fish or fish oil.

**Creatine Monohydrate:** The most research-supported performance supplement. 3-5g daily increases strength, power output, and muscle mass across all populations. No loading phase required. No cycling required.

## Meal Prep as Discipline Infrastructure

Nutrition compliance is 80% environment, 20% willpower. Members who meal prep spend 5-6 hours on Sunday to make 21+ meals. Members who don't prep make impulsive decisions 21 times per week.

FORGE teaches the foundational meal prep protocol:
1. **Sunday batch cook:** 5lb chicken breast or ground beef, 5lb white rice or sweet potatoes, 4 bags of vegetables
2. **Portion and store:** 6 containers with protein + carb + vegetable
3. **Pre-log in tracking app:** Remove the decision from the day
4. **Repeat:** The second and third weeks are faster than the first

A member who meal preps consistently cannot score below 60 on the nutrition score.

## Hydration for Performance

Dehydration of just 2% of bodyweight reduces strength output by 10-20% and increases perceived exertion at any given intensity. Members often attribute fatigue and poor performance to "not feeling it" when the real cause is inadequate hydration.

**Minimum baseline:** Half your bodyweight in ounces of water daily
**Training adjustment:** Add 16-24oz per hour of training
**Post-workout:** Add 20oz for every pound lost during training

FORGE messaging flags members who report low energy during training with a hydration reminder before prescribing nutritional interventions.

## The FORGE Weekly Nutrition Challenge

FORGE deploys one nutrition focus per week for the entire gym community. Simple, measurable, achievable.

- Week 1: Protein first — hit 1g/lb protein every day this week
- Week 2: Pre-workout fuel — eat a real meal 60-90 minutes before every session
- Week 3: Meal prep Sunday — 3-day meal prep, report compliance
- Week 4: Hydration audit — track water intake for 7 days, hit your target
